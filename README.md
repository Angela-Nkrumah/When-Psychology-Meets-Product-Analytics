# When Psychology Meets Product Analytics
### Identifying Behavioural Friction in SaaS Trial Conversion


## Overview

This project applies behavioural psychology frameworks; cognitive load theory, present bias, the Fogg behaviour model, and the early activation principle, to a synthetic SaaS trial dataset of 5,000 users to identify where and why trial users disengage before becoming paying customers.

The result is a transparent, explainable conversion risk scoring system that helps customer success and growth teams prioritise their resources toward users most likely to convert and intervene early with users at risk of churning.


## Key Findings
- Users who activate within the first 3 days convert at **2.6x** the rate of users who do not.
  
- Completing onboarding increases conversion from **9.7% to 38.1%**.
  
- Users who do both convert at **51.9%** vs **5.6%** for users who do neither.
  
- High cognitive load users show near-zero conversion, supporting Sweller's cognitive load theory.
  
- Rule-based risk scoring successfully separates users into segments ranging from **1.6%** to **95.2%** actual conversion rate.
